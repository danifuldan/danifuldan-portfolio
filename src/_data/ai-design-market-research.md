# AI Product Design Job Market Research — March 2026

## Key Findings

### General design market: oversaturated at junior/entry level
- Less than 5% of tech companies open to hiring entry-level design talent
- Designers securing a new job within 3 months dropped from 67.9% (2019) to 49.5% (2024)
- Senior/generalist roles recovering faster than entry-level

### AI-specific design roles: demand outpacing supply
- 79% of hiring managers report increasing need for candidates who can design AI products (Figma State of the Designer 2026)
- AI design roles sit unfilled for 60+ days on average (Robert Half 2026)
- AI skills carry 28% salary premium (~$18k/year) (Lightcast 2025)
- 1 in 3 designers say they are working on AI products (up 50% YoY)
- 51% of those are building agentic products (up from 21% prior year)
- Design job postings across Designer Fund's VC portfolio up ~60% in 2025 vs. 2024

### The differentiator is depth, not exposure
- Many designers can say "I worked on a product that uses AI"
- Far fewer can demonstrate designing for: model uncertainty, AI failure states, trust calibration, agent autonomy boundaries, multi-step agentic workflows
- 72% of designers use generative AI tools — but using tools ≠ designing AI products
- Most portfolios "read like they could belong to any product designer" (ADPList)
- NN/g 2026: "If you're just slapping together components from a design system, you're already replaceable by AI"

### Window is narrowing
- AI product design experience growing 50% YoY among designers
- The gap is real right now but closing as more designers ship AI-native products
